Adobe + Netflix Creative Jam

Participated in a cross-continental team of 3 in a two day design experience tournament organised by Adobe and Netflix using Adobe XD.



Problem Statement

Netflix invests in creating and giving a platform to stories that connect people and cultures from around the world. How might we help families and friends utilize this platform and share their experience with content such as series television shows and movies they love?

Design a third-party desktop web site or desktop app (not Netflix-branded) where the community can celebrate shared stories together through communal and authentic experiences.

Our approach

Stakeholders

First, we identified stakeholders. They are all users who are invested in watching movies, but major focus is on those who are looking for discussions, conversations with people who have the same tastes and having a fun time with family and friends when there is physical distance

Requirements

Based on the brief, we identified certain requirements. Additionally, since people are familiar with popular platforms like Slack, Linkedin, Spotify, Facebook and Amazon, we wanted to keep the best features from these platforms on ours, so it makes the transition easier

  • Watch content with family remotely and privately
  • Create anticipation by scheduling watch parties and pre-event discussion
  • Engage in real-time conversations before, during, or afterwards
  • Schedule and join small community and larger worldwide watch parties
  • Make and share lists of favorite content and content creators
  • Share relevant references to the content subject for further interest and education
  • Help connect people with more diverse content, cultures, locales, and subject matter
  • Encourage ratings and reviews, and share personal favorites with others
  • Highlight and make notes of favorite lines, chapters, and Characters
  • Discover content by way of other metadata, i.e. highest rated, release date, filming location, running time, writer, subplot, production company, etc

Important Questions

To understand the user and design the user experience, we needed to answer several questions. Due to lack of time, no onground user research or observation was done.

What is my first screen?

  • Your last watched
  • My personal calendar and upcoming schedule
  • Group activities
  • Friend activities
  • Search bar to search for people/movies/shows/groups

How do I search?

  • Search by name
  • Search for people based on location, matching wishlists, past watches, etc
  • Search for movies/TV Shows based on ratings, filiming locations, time duration, etc
  • Search for groups based on movie/TV Shows, friends who are members, etc

What do I see on clicking TV show/Movie

  • Option to watch the movie
  • Rating and reviews by people
  • Groups/channels associated with it and upcoming parties in the same
  • Friends who have watched it
  • Release date, filming location, running time, writer, subplot, production company
  • Fun facts related to the movie/show

What do I do when I am watching?

  • Have the option to add people to watch/chat/videocall with
  • React to scenes with emojis
  • Highlight good dialogues/scenes/episodes
  • Option to share as status
  • Leave comments and ratings post the episode/movie

Who do I want to watch it with?

  • Family and friends
  • Members of groups you are part of
  • Randomly decided by algorithm
  • Finding new people based on match%

What do I see when I click on a person’s profile?

  • Image
  • Name
  • Match%
  • Currently watching/last watched
  • Wishlist
  • List of favourite quotes/episodes/scenes

Where do I want to discuss?

  • One community associated with each movie/TV Show - each has a sub channel on different topics
  • Discuss while watching the movie with friends/family/group
  • On groups which have only pre-defined friends and family

What do I want to share?

  • What I am currently watching
  • Who I am watching with
  • Screenshot of scene
  • Videocall of watching
  • Personal watching calendar for the month

Wireframing

The minimum number of artboards required according to the design brief were 15. We wireframed the basic pages needed according to the reqirements and the questions answered above. Each page would have artboards for drop downs and hence we were confident we could cross the 15 artboard requirement

Pages

  • Home page
  • Profile Page
  • Movie introduction page
  • Watching a movie page
  • Search a movie page
  • Direct message page
  • Match with people page

Solution

With the increasing amount of time people spend watching movies online, there is an inherent desire to connect people from the comfort of your home, be it watching a movie remotely with friends and family or finding die-hard fans of your favourite series and bonding with them. Our product wishes to help you find people who have the same likes/interests as you, discuss movies while, before and after watching an episode and online watch parties with family and friends. The user experience is intuitive as it takes in the best features of the platforms in the market like LinkedIn, Facebook, Amazon, Spotify, Slack, Netflix-most of which are regularly used by people for different purposes. The visual design has been done in dark mode by default to reduce eye strain. USPs include each movie/TV Show having a dedicated group with various channels to streamline discussion, matching with people around the world based on %matching preferences, being able to chat and video-call while watching a movie in sync and a calendar system to keep track of all parties, events and activities.
